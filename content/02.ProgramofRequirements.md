# Functions and Program of Requirements of the System
This is the program of requirements for the entire system. The goal is to specify the features and functionalities of the theremin. The requirements are divided in ten different subjects.


## 1. Educational Value

### Core Learning Objectives
- The device must enable students to observe and manipulate clearly distinguishable frequencies across at least 4 octaves.
- The device must enable students to observe and manipulate at least 3 noticeable volume levels.
- The device must enable students to observe and manipulate at least 3 distinct overtones.

### Demonstrability
- Each parameter must be independently adjustable (e.g., separate controls for pitch and volume).

### Feedback
- The device must provide real-time feedback (audio and visual).
- Input-to-sound latency must be < 30 ms (feels instantaneous).

### Learning Linkage
- Each concept must be explainable using terms from the existing learning module (e.g., Hz, waveform, sine wave, harmonics).

### Simplicity
- A new user must be able to produce sound within 10 seconds without instruction.
- Total number of controls: ≤ 10 primary inputs (e.g., knobs, buttons, sliders).

---

## 2. Curiosity & Engagement ("Magical" Interaction)

### Discoverability
The device should allow at least one "unexpected" or surprising effect, such as:
- Gesture-controlled pitch
- Visuals reacting to sound

### Exploration Curiosity
- Students should become curious about the concepts of the learning module through the device’s interaction.

### Interaction Style
- The device should have touchless interaction (e.g., proximity sensors).
- The device should have gesture-based control.
- The device should visually respond to user inputs.

---

## 3. Connection to Existing Educational Module

### Concept Mapping
- Each feature must map to at least one curriculum concept.

### Teacher Usability
- Must be explainable by a teacher in ≤ 5 minutes after reading a manual.

### Abstraction Level
- Students should be able to grasp the concepts at a level of abstraction that fits within the existing learning module.

---

## 4. Ease of Use

### Accessibility
- The device should be usable by left- and right-handed users.
- Users should be able to play the device without any musical or technical experience.

### Interaction Clarity
- Each control must have a single function.
- Each control should have a visual or physical label.

### Setup Time
- The device must be ready to use within ≤ 1 minute of turning on the device.

### Error Tolerance
- No controlled usage should break the device or require a reset procedure beyond a simple power cycle.

---

## 5. Cost Constraints

### Target Cost per Unit
- The prototype should cost less than €300.
- A mass-producible classroom version should cost less than €150 per unit.

### Component Strategy
- The device should be built using off-the-shelf components and widely available microcontrollers (e.g., Arduino-class).

### Maintenance
- Replacement parts should cost less than 20% of the total device cost.

---

## 6. Ease of Development (8-Week BAP Constraint)

### Prototype Feasibility
- The group must achieve a functional prototype within 8 weeks that generates a controllable sound.

### Technical Scope
- The project must be achievable with basic microcontroller programming and basic circuit design.
- The project must be achievable with standard sensors (e.g., potentiometers, distance sensors, buttons).

### Complexity Limits
- The project should avoid custom PCB design.
- The project should avoid advanced signal processing beyond basic waveform synthesis.

---

## 7. Physical Requirements

### Size
- The device should not be smaller than 25 × 20 × 10 cm.
- The device should not be larger than 40 × 30 × 20 cm.

### Weight
- The device should weigh less than 2 kg.

### Portability
- The device should be liftable and carriable by one person.
- The device should include appropriate carrying features (e.g., handle, grip surfaces, or compact form factor).

### Durability
- The device must withstand frequent classroom handling.
- The device must withstand minor drops from a height of 50 cm.

### Power
- The device should be USB-powered or battery-powered with at least 4 hours of runtime.

### Sound Output
- The device should have a built-in speaker with a safe classroom volume of 85 dB at 1 meter.

---

## 8. Additional Criteria

### Safety
- There should be no exposed wires on the device.
- The device should not have sharp edges.
- The device should be stable enough that it does not tip over.

### Scalability
- The design should allow for easy replication (documentation + parts list).
- The device should be suitable for batch assembly.

### Repairability
- The design should allow repairs to be made with basic tools.

---

## 9. Success Criteria

- A student must be able to explain pitch vs. loudness after using the device.
- A student must be able to intentionally demonstrate at least 2 sound changes.
- A teacher must be able to integrate the device into a lesson without extra preparation.
- The device must survive a full classroom session without failure.
- The design should stay within budget and time constraints.
