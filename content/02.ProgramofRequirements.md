# General Requirements

## 1. Educational Value

### Core Learning Objectives

The device must enable students to observe and manipulate:

- **Frequency:** clearly distinguishable across at least 4 octaves
- **Amplitude:** at least 3 noticeable loudness levels
- **Overtones/Timbre:** at least 3 distinct sound "colors"

### Demonstrability

- Each parameter must be independently adjustable (e.g., separate controls for pitch and volume).

### Feedback

- The device must provide real-time feedback (audio and visual).
- Input-to-sound latency must be **< 100 ms** (feels instantaneous).

### Learning Linkage

- Each concept must be explainable using terms from the existing learning module (e.g., Hz, waveform, sine wave, harmonics).

### Simplicity

- A new user must be able to produce sound within 10 seconds without instruction.
- Total number of controls: **≤ 10** primary inputs (e.g., knobs, buttons, sliders).

---

## 2. Curiosity & Engagement ("Magical" Interaction)

### Discoverability

The device should allow at least one unexpected or surprising effect, such as:

- Gesture-controlled pitch
- Visuals reacting to sound

### Exploration Curiosity

- Students should become curious about the concepts of the learning module through the device's interaction.

### Interaction Style

Preferred:

- Touchless interaction (e.g., proximity sensors)
- Gesture-based control
- Visually responsive elements (LEDs, moving parts)

---

## 3. Connection to Existing Educational Module

### Concept Mapping

- Each feature must map to at least one curriculum concept.

### Teacher Usability

- Must be explainable by a teacher in **≤ 5 minutes** after reading a manual.

### Abstraction Level

- Students should be able to grasp concepts at a level of abstraction that fits within the existing learning module.
- Below this abstraction level, the concepts can be much more complicated.

---

## 4. Ease of Use

### Accessibility

Usable by:

- Left- and right-handed users
- Users with no prior musical or technical experience

### Interaction Clarity

Each control must have:

- A single clear function
- Visual or physical labeling

### Setup Time

- Device must be ready to use within **≤ 1 minute** of being powered on.

### Error Tolerance

No control usage should:

- Break the device
- Require reset procedures beyond a simple power cycle

---

## 5. Cost Constraints

### Target Cost per Unit

- **Prototype:** ≤ €300
- **Mass-producible classroom version:** ≤ €150 per unit

### Classroom Deployment

- Ratio: 1 device per 10 students

### Component Strategy

Preferred:

- Off-the-shelf components
- Widely available microcontrollers (e.g., Arduino-class)

### Maintenance

- Replacement parts cost **≤ 20%** of the total device cost.

---

## 6. Ease of Development (8-Week BAP Constraint)

### Prototype Feasibility

Must achieve a functional prototype within 8 weeks, including:

- Sound generation
- At least 2 controllable parameters (e.g., pitch + volume)

### Technical Scope

Must be achievable with:

- Basic microcontroller programming
- Standard sensors (potentiometers, distance sensors, buttons)
- Basic circuit design
- Basic audio output (speaker or headphone jack)

### Complexity Limits

Avoid:

- Custom PCB (optional, not required)
- Advanced DSP beyond basic waveform synthesis

---

## 7. Physical Requirements

### Size

Approximate dimensions:

- Between **25 × 20 × 10 cm** and **40 × 30 × 20 cm**

### Weight

- ≤ 2 kg per device

### Portability

- Must be liftable and carriable by one person.
- Should include carrying features (e.g., handle, grip surfaces, or compact form factor).

### Durability

Must withstand:

- Minor drops (≈ 50 cm height)
- Frequent classroom handling

### Power

Options:

- USB-powered, **or**
- Battery-powered with **≥ 4 hours** runtime

### Sound Output

- Built-in speaker with safe classroom volume
- Maximum approximately **85 dB at 1 meter**

---

## 8. Additional Criteria

### Safety

- No exposed wiring
- No sharp edges
- Stable enough not to fall when placed on a table

### Scalability

Design should allow:

- Easy replication (documentation + parts list)
- Batch assembly

### Repairability

- Design should allow repairs using basic tools.

---

## 9. Success Criteria

### A Student Can

- Explain pitch versus loudness after using the device
- Demonstrate at least 2 intentional sound changes

### A Teacher Can

- Integrate it into a lesson without extra preparation

### The Device

- Survives a full classroom session without failure
- Stays within budget and time constraints
