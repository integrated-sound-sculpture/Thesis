This BAP project works towards a functional sound sculpture that serves next to an NLT module to excite students. A sound sculpture should draw attention and invite the user to interact with it. This interaction should yield an audible output.

The NLT module addresses topics such as sound waves and frequency. The sound sculpture should give an interactive and or intuitive way to learn about these concepts.

[](#fig-SympleSystem) shows the sub-systems of a sound sculpture. First, user interacts, which should be sensed. The resulting signal should be processed and turned into an audible sound by the sound generator.

## Problem Definition
Existing musical instruments do not provide a simple and intuitive way to connect
interactive user inputs with corresponding audio and visual feedback for educational purposes. This makes it difficult for VWO 5/6 students to understand how sound properties are influenced by physical interaction.

## Goal
Creating a tool for teaching and exciting VWO 5/6 students for STEM by letting them interact with a sound sculpture and supporting relevant (already existing) learning modules.

## Project Structure
As a baseline for approaching solving the given problem the engineering design method is used, with some additions and limitations. Due to the limited time of the project it is unwise to use an iterative prototyping method, therefore the focus is laid on the process of making concepts and alternatives. These three will make making choices structured and fit within the time bound of the project. The actual process is then 


1. **Problem Definition**
    Clearly define the problem, objectives, and constraints.
1. **Background Research**
    Gather relevant technical knowledge, benchmarks, and existing solutions.
1. **Global Requirements Definition**
    Establish system-level requirements (functional, performance, constraints).
1. **Concept Generation**
    Develop multiple high-level concepts and solution approaches.
1. **Concept Evaluation \& Selection**
    Compare concepts using criteria (requirements, feasibility, risk) and select the most promising direction.
1. **System Architecture Definition**
    Break the system into two major subsystems and define their interactions.
1. **Subsystem Decomposition**
    Assign clear boundaries and responsibilities to each subsystem.
1. **Subsystem Requirements Specification**
    Define detailed requirements for each subsystem based on global requirements.
1. **Alternative Development per Subsystem**
    Generate and evaluate multiple implementation options for each subsystem.
1. **Implementation Selection**
    Choose solutions using requirements-driven decision methods (e.g., trade-off matrices).
1. **Prototype Development (Single Iteration Focus)**
    Build a prototype aimed at validating the most critical assumptions.
1. **Testing \& Validation**
    Test against requirements and identify major gaps or risks.
