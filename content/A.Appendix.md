# Ethical Implications of the Product and Engineering Process

## Societal impacts
The project will likely have a lot of positive impacts on multiple stakeholders:

- **Students:** The students will be the users of the interactive sound sculpture. This project will help them to improve on the physics behind (sound)waves and the electronics that are used
to produce sound. Additionally it would make the courses more fun. This could help them with passing their courses.

- **Physics Teachers:** The teachers will use the instrument during their lessons. It will make it easier for the teachers to teach difficult subject regarding sound waves, because they now have a good tool to visualize the waves. 

- **High Schools:** The interactive sound sculpture will be used on secondary schools to help
with the education of the students. The school will benefit from this project, because it will
help them improving their education. This could lead to an increase in the graduating rates
and an increase in registrations. 

- **TU Delft:** The university will benefit from an educational program focused on children to
make them enthusiastic about STEM related topics. The TU Delft makes money and space
available for this project. In return, this could lead to an increase of registrations to the
university.

However, the project may also have some negative impacts on society.

Not all schools may be able to afford the learning device. This could increase educational inequality, as students at these schools would have fewer learning opportunities than those at more privileged schools.

The electronics used in the theremin require raw materials, which can have environmental impacts in the areas where these materials are mined. In addition, the theremin will contribute to electronic waste. Since much of this waste is not properly recycled, it can create further environmental problems.

## Ethical Aspects of development
During the development process, we collected feedback and requirements from teachers and pedagogues to better understand the educational value and usability of the device. This data collection introduced ethical considerations regarding the privacy of the participants. Although the information gathered was primarily related to professional opinions rather than sensitive personal data, it was still important to store and handle the responses responsibly

Designing the device required the use of various electronic components, sensors, circuit boards, and construction materials. As some components became obsolete or unusable during the iterative design process, material waste was generated. This raises environmental concerns because electronic waste contains valuable resources as well as substances that can be harmful if not disposed of properly.

## Ethical evaluation
The genuine ethical aspects that arose during the design process are:

- **Sustainability:** The device should try to minimize electronic waste and resource consumption. Furthermore, the device should not consume a lot of energy since (electric) energy is a scarce commodity in these times that cannot be taken for granted
- **Educational fairness:** The device should be affordable by all school so it does not just benefit the the more privileged schools. 

These two aspects are competing values. When the device is more affordable for underprivileged schools the device will also be of lower quality. This will mean that the device  breaks earlier, which will lead to more electronic waste. It is therefore essential to chose a middle ground between affordability and quality.
