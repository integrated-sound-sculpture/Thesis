# Hardware & Sensing Subsystem Overview
As touch up on in the [](03.5Overview.md) chapter, the hardware and sensing subgroup is responsible for the interactions between the user and the sounds sculpture. This subgroup covers the sensing, visualization and support systems of the project. This chapter will apply [the general programme of requirements](02.2General_Requirements.md) to the hardware and sensing submodule.

## Functions
The hardware and sensing submodule must take care of the following functions of the sound sculpture:

1. Measure the hand position of the instrument’s performer.
2. Communicate with the microcontroller.
3. Output audible audio through a speaker.
4. Select the waveform of the audio.
5. Supply power.
6. Select the volume of the audio.
7. Be visually attractive.
8. Support calibration.
9. Select automatic mapping of musical notes.
10. Visualize the output.

To achieve these functions the submodule is divided into three separate parts:

* Sensing
This part of the sub-module focusses on the contactless sensing method, oscillator and the digital-to-analog converter.

* Visualization
This part of the sub-module focusses on a method of visualisation of the Theremin.

* Support Systems
This part of the sub-module focusses on all other hardware of the project, including all input systems, led systems and the housing of the project.

## Requirements
### Functional Requirements

1. The device must provide the possibility to select between three different waveforms (sinusoidal, block, and sawtooth wave).
2. The device must be able to adjust the volume from 0% to 100% of the amplifier's maximum output.
3. The latency of the hardware shall not exceed 50 ms.
4. The device shall provide a visual representation of the waveforms.
5. The device shall include an integrated speaker system for audio output.

### Curiosity & Engagement ("Magical" Interaction)

1. The device must include a contactless sensing method for detecting the user's hand during interaction.
2. The housing of the device shall enable intuitive interaction by the user.
3. The housing of the device shall be aesthetically appealing to the target audience.

### Ease of Use

1. All inputs shall be clearly labeled on the housing.
2. The housing of the device shall withstand normal user wear.

### Cost Constraints

1. The hardware cost of the end product shall not exceed €120.

### Ease of Development

1. The internal hardware shall be created using a basic circuit design with commonly available electrical components.

### Physical Requirements

1. The housing size, excluding a possible antenna, shall not exceed 40 × 30 × 20 cm.
2. The total weight of the device shall not exceed 2 kg.
3. The housing shall include appropriate carrying features.
4. The housing must withstand minor impacts and scratches from normal use.
5. The speaker must produce audible sound that does not exceed 85 dB when measured at a distance of 1 meter.
6. The device must operate using a standard power supply (e.g., power socket or USB-C).
7. The device shall be portable by ensuring that protruding parts can either fold in or be removed and carried separately.

### Compatibility

1. The system must be able to communicate with the selected microcontroller.
2. The system must supply the operating voltage required by the microcontroller.

### Additional Criteria

1. There shall be no external wiring.
2. The housing shall not have sharp edges or corners that may cause injury.
3. The device shall remain stable while placed on a flat surface during operation.
4. The design shall allow access for maintenance and possible repairs.
5. The device must be electrically insulated and comply with standard safety regulations to protect the user from high-voltage hazards.
