# Sensing
The sensing module of the system is required to make the device fun and interactive. The module is required to do the following:
1. Sense the distance between the player's hand and the sensor
2. Make use of contactless sensing
3. Be intuitive to use
4. Output the results

## Design exploration
To start design the sensing module, multiple sensing methods were evaluated to find the most suitable approach to sensing the player's hand. The main requirement was to reliably detect the players hand without making physical contact with the device, all while remaining intuitive for the player to use.
The following methods were evaluated:
1. Ultrasonic sensing
2. Lidar sensing
3. Capacitive sensing

### Ultrasonic sensing
An ultrasonic sensor uses high frequency sound waves and measures the time of flight of the reflected signal.
$$ d = \frac{v_{sound} t_{flight}}{2}$$
Using the speed of sound it converts the time to distance. This is a pretty reliable method for sensing moderate distances. 

However there are some drawbacks with ultrasonic sensing. Due to the sensor being limited to the speed of sound, there is some delay introduced in the measurement. The delay introduced by an object at a distance of $50cm$ is already $3ms$, which would only increase when the measurement is processed by the sensor. This would result in a decrease in the responsitivity of the sound sculpture, which would decrease the interactiveness. 

Another drawback is the interference from other sensors. If multiple sound sculptures were played in the same room, the sensors could detect sound waves from other sensors giving a very inaccurate measurement.

And finally the accuracy of ultrasonic sensors is very much limited at close range. Since the sound sculpture needs to be interactive, the distance between the hand and the player is usally in this region (less then $50cm$)

That is why ultrasonic sensing was not used in the design.

### Lidar sensing.

Lidar (Light Detection And Ranging) is another method to remotely measure distance, and, like ultrasonic, also another method that uses time of fligth in its measurement. The main difference is that Lidar uses a beam of light instead of a sound wave.

Lidar has a very good range, typically around 40 meters, and accuracy, hence why it is used in many applications.

However Lidar sensors are quite expensive due to their internal complexity. Another flaw is that most Lidar sensors are optimzed for longe range used and not for close range measurements like in our application. 

### Capacitive sensing

Capacitive sensing is the final and most unique method that was evaluated. It relies on a cpacitor being created between an antenna and an external conductive object, the players hand in our case. 

Capacitive sensing is very suitable for close range applications, since a small distance results in a  larger capacitance and thus a better sensitivity when played. This method is also very cheap since any conductive material can be used as an antenna.

Another big advantage is that due to capacitor being formed between the player and the antenna, the angular position of the hand does not matter, only the absolute distance. This creates a 3D enviroment for the player to play in. This would be more interactive then the other sensing methods. 

The downside however is that the antenna also forms a capacitor with the rest of the enviroment, not only the players hand. This would lead to inconsistency in the frequency of the oscillator. The solution is to add a form of calibration, but this would increase the complexity.

In the end capacitive sensing was chosen as the approach for the sensing module.


## Operating principle

### Capacitive sensing principle
Capacitive sensing is the way the original theremin used to sense the player's hand position. This was done by making use of an oscillator. This oscillator consisted of inductive and capacitive elements. By placing an antenna in parallel with the other capacitive elements, it would create one plate of a capacitor. The idea behind this method is that the other plate is formed by the player's hand. This principle relies on the fact that the human body is for a large part made up of salt water, which is a conductor.  Since the resistance of the rest of the human body is so large, the plate formed by the human hand is effectively grounded, making the player part of the circuit.
The general formula for capacitance between two conducting plates is given by:

$$    C = \frac{\epsilon A}{d} $$

Where $A$ is the overlapping area of the two "plates", $\epsilon$ the permittivity and $d$ the distance. In this practical case, there are two things that affect the capacitance: the area and the distance. From an increase in overlapping area follows an increase in capacitance. And the closer the two plates are to each other, the greater the capacitance. 

### Oscillator principle
The circuti below shows a simple LC circuit that demonstrates the workings of an oscillator. It is made up of a capacitor, and inductor and a voltage source which is turned on after $t = 0$

:::{figure} figures/lc-circuit1-schem
:name: schem1
Schematic of an LC circuit with total capacitance of 10nF
:::

This circuit results in an osciallating signal due to the capacitor and the inductor constantly charging and discharging. 

The frequency of the waveform is dependand on the capacitance and inductance. This is given by the general formula:
$$ 
f_0 = \frac{1}{2 \pi \sqrt{LC}}
$$ 

This works out to be: $f = \frac{1}{2 \pi \sqrt{1 \cdot 10^{-8}}} = \space 1.592 kHz$. So we expect to see a frequency of around $1.6kHz$ in this circuit.

### Effect of the hand
Now if an antenna would be connected to the capacitor, it would act as an extra capacitor added in parallel. This example assumes a capacitance of 10 nF between the player and the antenna. This is illustrated in the figure below.
:::{figure} figures/lc-circuit2-schem
:name: lc-circ-2
The same LC-circuit but with a capacitor added in parallel, resembling the antenna.
:::
The extra capacitor added increases the total capacitance of the circuit to $20 nF$. Usign the same formula as before, the frequency of oscillation should decrease: $f = \frac{1}{2 \pi \sqrt{2 \cdot 10^{-8}}} = \space 1.125 kHz.$

This principle is applied and used together with an antenna to create a signal that is dependand on the player's hand position.

:::{figure} figures/lc2-fft

The FFT of the circuit with the antenna connected. The peak is now at f = 1.1kHz
:::

So indeed, an increase in capacitance leads to a lower frequency of oscillation.
